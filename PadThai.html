Pad Thai

Home

Pad Thai is a stir-fried rice noodle dish commonly served as a street food and at casual local eateries in Thailand. It is made with soaked dried rice noodles, which are stir-fried with eggs and chopped firm tofu, and flavored with tamarind pulp, fish sauce, dried shrimp, garlic or shallots, red chili pepper and palm sugar, and served with lime wedges and often chopped roasted peanuts.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz. (225g) dried rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 oz. (225g) shrimp, chicken, or extra firm tofu, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 3 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons crushed peanuts
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Steps

  1. Soak the rice noodles in hot water for about 8-10 minutes, or until they are soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and stir-fry for about 30 seconds, or until aromatic.
  3. Add the shrimp, chicken, or tofu to the wok and stir-fry until cooked through.
  4. Push the ingredients to one side of the wok and crack the egg into the other side. Scramble the egg until cooked, then mix it in with the other ingredients.
  5. Add the soaked rice noodles to the wok, along with the bean sprouts, bell pepper, and green onions. Stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes, or until the noodles are heated through.
  6. Remove the wok from the heat and stir in the Pad Thai sauce until everything is well coated.
  7. Divide the Pad Thai between plates and top with crushed peanuts and a squeeze of lime juice. Serve hot and enjoy!
